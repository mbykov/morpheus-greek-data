STRONGS NT 98: Ἀδραμυττηνός

Ἀδραμυττηνός, (ή, , of Adramyttium (Ἀδραμύττιον, Ἀδραμύττειον, Ἀδραμμύτειον (also Ἀτραμμυτ., etc., cf. Poppo, Thucydides part i, vol. ii., p. 441f; Wetstein (1752) on Acts, as below; WH Ἀδραμυντηνός, cf. their Introductory § 408 and Appendix, p. 160)), a seaport of Mysia: Acts 27:2 (modern Edremit, Ydramit, Adramiti, etc.; cf. McClintock and Strong's Cyclopaedia under the word ).
