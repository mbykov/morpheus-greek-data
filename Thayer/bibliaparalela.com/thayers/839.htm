Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 839: αὔριον

αὔριον, adverb (from ἀυρο the morning air, and this from αὔω to breathe, blow; (according to others akin to ἠώς, Latinaurora; Curtius, § 613, cf. Vanicek, p. 944)), tomorrow (Latincras): Matthew 6:30; Luke 12:28; Acts 23:15 Rec., ; ; 1 Corinthians 15:32 (from Isaiah 22:13); σήμερον καί αὔριον, Luke 13:32; James 4:13 (Rec.st G, others σήμερον ἤ αὔριον). ἡ αὔριον namely, ἡμέρα (Winers Grammar, § 64, 5; Buttmann, § 123, 8) the morrow, Matthew 6:34; Acts 4:3; ἐπί τήν αὔριον, on the morrow, i. e. the next morning, Luke 10:35; Acts 4:5; τό (L τά; WH omits) τῆς αὔριον, what the morrow will bring forth, James 4:14. (From Homer down.)
