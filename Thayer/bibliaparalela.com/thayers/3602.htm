Thayer's Greek Lexicon
STRONGS NT 3602: ὀδυρμός

ὀδυρμός, ὀδυρμοῦ, ὁ (ὀδύρομαι to wail, lament (see κλαίω, at the end)), a wailing, lamentation, mourning: Matthew 2:18 (from Jeremiah 38:15 () for תַּמְרוּרִים); 2 Corinthians 7:7. (2 Macc. 11:6; Aeschylus, Euripides, Plato, Josephus, Plutarch, Aelian v. h. 14, 22.)
